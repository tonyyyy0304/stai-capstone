# Payroll and Compensation FAQ

## Pay Schedule

Salaries are paid semi-monthly: on the 15th and on the last calendar day of each month. When a payday falls on a weekend or holiday, payment is released on the last working day before it. Each payout covers the half-month period ending five (5) days before the payday to allow for timekeeping cutoff and processing.

| Payday | Period Covered | Timekeeping Cutoff |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 15th of the month | 26th of previous month – 10th | 10th |
| Last day of the month | 11th – 25th | 25th |

## Payslips

Electronic payslips are published in the HRIS on every payday. Payslips itemize basic pay, allowances, overtime, night differential, government contributions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG), withholding tax, and other deductions. Printed or certified payslips for loan or visa applications can be requested from HR with three (3) working days lead time.

## 13th Month Pay

All rank-and-file employees receive 13th month pay equal to one-twelfth (1/12) of total basic salary earned within the calendar year, released on or before December 24, in compliance with PD 851. Employees who join mid-year receive a prorated amount based on months worked. Employees who resign before December receive their prorated 13th month pay as part of final pay.

## Overtime and Premium Pay

Overtime must be pre-approved by your manager in the HRIS. Rates for non-exempt (rank-and-file) employees follow the Labor Code:

| Situation | Rate |
| --- | --- |
| Overtime on a regular workday | 125% of hourly rate |
| Work on a rest day or special non-working day | 130% (plus 30% on hours beyond 8) |
| Work on a regular holiday | 200% |
| Night differential (10:00 PM – 6:00 AM) | +10% of hourly rate |

Managerial and supervisory employees are exempt from overtime pay but may receive compensatory time off at their Department Head's discretion.

## Salary Reviews and Promotions

Merit-based salary reviews happen once a year, effective April 1, following the annual performance cycle. Promotions may occur at any time but are typically decided during the April and October talent review windows. Salary adjustments are reflected in the first full payroll cycle after the effective date.

## Deductions and Loans

Standard deductions include the employee shares of SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG contributions, and withholding tax. Voluntary deductions — SSS or Pag-IBIG salary loans, HMO dependent premiums, provident fund contributions — are itemized on the payslip. Payroll errors should be reported to payroll@luminaworks.example within five (5) working days of payday; corrections are made in the next cycle, or by off-cycle payment if the error exceeds twenty percent (20%) of net pay.

## Final Pay

Employees who separate from the company receive final pay within thirty (30) to forty-five (45) calendar days after their last working day, provided clearance is completed. Final pay includes unpaid salary, prorated 13th month pay, converted unused vacation leave (per the Leave Policy), and tax refund if any, less accountabilities. The Certificate of Employment is issued within three (3) working days of request, independent of clearance status.

## Tax Documents

BIR Form 2316 is issued to all employees by January 31 for the preceding year, and to separated employees together with their final pay. For questions about tax computation, contact payroll@luminaworks.example.
