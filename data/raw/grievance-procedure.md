# Grievance and Complaint Procedure

## Purpose and Scope

This procedure gives every employee a consistent, tracked way to raise workplace concerns: disputes with colleagues or managers, working conditions, payroll or benefits errors that ordinary channels have not resolved, policy violations, and perceived unfair treatment. Complaints involving harassment, discrimination, safety threats, or suspected illegal activity follow this procedure but are additionally governed by the Anti-Harassment and Disciplinary Policy and are escalated immediately to a human HR officer — they are never handled by automated triage alone.

## How to File a Complaint

Complaints may be filed through any of the following channels; all reach the same HR case queue:

1. The HR chatbot or HR service portal (creates a tracked ticket immediately)
2. Email to grievance@luminaworks.example
3. In person with any HR Business Partner
4. Through your manager, who must forward it to HR within one (1) working day

Choosing one channel over another does not affect how the complaint is handled.

## Required Information

To open a case, provide as much of the following as you can. A complaint is not rejected for missing details, but complete information speeds up resolution:

- Category of the complaint (e.g., interpersonal conflict, payroll, policy violation, harassment, safety)
- Description of what happened, in your own words
- Names of people or teams involved
- Date(s) and location of the incident(s)
- Any supporting documents or witnesses
- The outcome you are seeking

## Process and Timelines

| Step | Timeline |
| --- | --- |
| Acknowledgement of the complaint | Within two (2) working days of filing |
| Assignment to a case handler | Within three (3) working days |
| Initial meeting with the complainant | Within five (5) working days |
| Investigation and resolution target | Within fifteen (15) working days of filing |
| Written outcome communicated | Within three (3) working days of resolution |

Complex cases may exceed the fifteen-day target; the case handler must inform the complainant of the delay and the revised timeline in writing.

## Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation

Complaints are handled on a strict need-to-know basis. Details are shared only with the people required to investigate and resolve the case. Retaliation against anyone who files a complaint in good faith, or participates in an investigation, is itself a serious offense subject to discipline up to dismissal. A complaint made in good faith that turns out to be unfounded is not penalized; complaints proven to be knowingly false may be subject to discipline.

## Anonymous Complaints

Anonymous complaints are accepted through the ethics hotline (hotline@luminaworks.example) and are investigated to the extent the information allows. Note that anonymity can limit the investigation and prevents us from updating you on the outcome. Where possible, we encourage named complaints under the confidentiality protections above.

## Appeals

If you disagree with the outcome, you may appeal in writing to the HR Director within seven (7) working days of receiving the written outcome. Appeals are reviewed by an appeals panel of three (3): the HR Director, a Department Head not in the complainant's chain, and an employee representative. The panel's decision, issued within ten (10) working days, is final within the company and does not limit any rights under Philippine labor law, including recourse to DOLE.

## Status Inquiries

You may check the status of your complaint at any time by quoting your ticket number through the HR chatbot, the service portal, or your case handler. Status values are: Filed, Acknowledged, Under Investigation, Resolved, and Closed.
