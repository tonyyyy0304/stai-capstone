# Leave Policy

## Vacation Leave

### Accrual

All regular full-time employees earn paid vacation leave based on tenure. Leave accrues monthly and is credited on the first working day of each month. Probationary employees begin accruing vacation leave from their start date but may only use it after regularization.

| Tenure | Annual Vacation Leave | Monthly Accrual |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0–2 years | 15 days | 1.25 days |
| 3–5 years | 18 days | 1.50 days |
| 6–9 years | 21 days | 1.75 days |
| 10+ years | 25 days | 2.08 days |

### Scheduling and Approval

Vacation leave must be requested through the HRIS at least five (5) working days in advance for leaves of 1–2 days, and at least ten (10) working days in advance for leaves of 3 or more consecutive days. Approval is at the discretion of your immediate manager, who will consider team coverage and project deadlines. Leave requests during declared company blackout periods (typically the last two weeks of a fiscal quarter) require Department Head approval.

### Carry-Over and Conversion

A maximum of five (5) unused vacation leave days may be carried over into the next calendar year. Carried-over days must be used by March 31 or they are forfeited. Unused vacation leave beyond the 5-day carry-over cap is automatically converted to cash at the end of the year, computed at the employee's daily basic rate as of December 1.

## Sick Leave

### Entitlement

Regular employees are entitled to fifteen (15) days of paid sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave does not carry over and is not convertible to cash. Sick leave may be used for personal illness, injury, medical appointments, and the care of an immediate family member who is ill.

### Documentation

Sick leaves of one (1) to two (2) consecutive days require only a notification to your manager via the HRIS or by message before 10:00 AM on the first day of absence. Sick leaves of three (3) or more consecutive days require a medical certificate from a licensed physician, submitted to HR within three (3) working days of returning to work. Failure to submit the certificate converts the absence to leave without pay.

### Extended Medical Leave

Employees who exhaust their sick leave due to a serious medical condition may apply for extended medical leave of up to sixty (60) calendar days without pay, with SSS sickness benefit assistance processed by HR. Positions are held for the duration of an approved extended medical leave.

## Parental Leave

### Maternity Leave

Female employees are entitled to one hundred five (105) calendar days of paid maternity leave per pregnancy in accordance with the Expanded Maternity Leave Law (RA 11210), with an option to extend for an additional thirty (30) days without pay. Solo mothers are entitled to an additional fifteen (15) paid days. Notify HR at least thirty (30) days before your expected leave date where possible.

### Paternity Leave

Married male employees are entitled to seven (7) working days of paid paternity leave for each of the first four (4) deliveries of their lawful spouse, per RA 8187. Paternity leave must be used within sixty (60) days of the child's birth.

### Solo Parent Leave

Employees holding a valid Solo Parent ID are entitled to seven (7) working days of parental leave per year after one (1) year of service, per RA 8972 as amended by RA 11861.

## Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to five (5) working days of paid bereavement leave upon the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling, grandparent, or parent-in-law), and two (2) working days for extended family (aunt, uncle, first cousin). Bereavement leave must be taken within thirty (30) days of the death. Supporting documentation (death certificate or obituary) should be provided to HR upon return.

## Leave Without Pay

Leave without pay (LWOP) may be granted for personal reasons at management's discretion when all applicable paid leave has been exhausted. LWOP exceeding thirty (30) consecutive calendar days requires Department Head and HR Director approval. Benefits accrual (vacation and sick leave) is suspended during LWOP.

## How to File a Leave Request

1. Log in to the HRIS portal and select "File Leave".
2. Choose the leave type, dates, and reason.
3. Submit for manager approval; you will receive a notification once acted on.
4. For sick leave, attach the medical certificate to the same request when required.

Emergency leave (sudden illness or family emergency) may be filed retroactively within two (2) working days of returning to work.
