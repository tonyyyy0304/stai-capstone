# Anti-Harassment and Disciplinary Policy

## Policy Statement

Lumina Works maintains zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, and violence in the workplace, in client sites, at company events, and in online channels used for work. This policy implements the Anti-Sexual Harassment Act (RA 7877) and the Safe Spaces Act (RA 11313), and applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties interacting with our staff.

## Definitions

### Harassment

Unwelcome conduct — verbal, physical, or online — that demeans, humiliates, intimidates, or creates a hostile work environment. Examples include slurs, mockery of a person's identity or background, persistent unwanted teasing, threatening behavior, cyberbullying in work channels, and abuse of authority such as public humiliation of a subordinate.

### Sexual Harassment

Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature, including: gender-based online harassment; sending unsolicited sexually explicit messages or images; unwanted touching; persistent unwanted invitations; and conditioning any employment benefit on a sexual favor. Under the Safe Spaces Act, peer-to-peer and subordinate-to-superior acts are covered — sexual harassment is not limited to acts by a person in authority.

### Discrimination

Adverse treatment on the basis of sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, age, religion, disability, ethnicity, civil status, pregnancy, union membership, or health condition (including HIV status per RA 11166), in any employment decision or workplace interaction.

## Reporting and Immediate Escalation

Reports may be raised through any channel in the Grievance and Complaint Procedure. Regardless of channel, all reports alleging harassment, sexual harassment, discrimination, violence, or threats to personal safety are escalated to a human HR officer within twenty-four (24) hours of receipt — automated systems (including the HR chatbot) must never triage, adjudicate, or close such reports on their own. In situations of immediate physical danger, contact building security (local 911 from any desk phone) or emergency services first, then inform HR.

## Committee on Decorum and Investigation (CODI)

Sexual harassment complaints are investigated by the CODI, constituted per RA 7877 with representation from management, rank-and-file employees, and the union or employee council. The CODI:

1. Receives the written complaint and notifies the respondent within three (3) working days
2. Gives the respondent five (5) calendar days to submit a written answer
3. Conducts hearings, keeping complainant and respondent separated on request
4. Issues findings and recommended sanctions within twenty (20) working days of the complaint

Interim measures — schedule changes, seating changes, preventive suspension of the respondent for up to thirty (30) days — may be applied while the investigation is ongoing. Preventive suspension is not a penalty.

## Sanctions

| Offense (first occurrence) | Sanction range |
| --- | --- |
| Offensive jokes/remarks after prior counseling | Written warning to 5-day suspension |
| Persistent unwelcome advances | 15-day suspension to dismissal |
| Sexual favors as condition of employment benefit | Dismissal |
| Physical harassment or assault | Dismissal and referral to authorities |
| Retaliation against a complainant or witness | 15-day suspension to dismissal |

Sanctions consider severity, position of authority, and prior record. Criminal liability under RA 7877, RA 11313, or the Revised Penal Code is independent of company discipline.

## Support for Complainants

Complainants may request: a change in reporting line or seating during investigation, paid administrative leave of up to five (5) days for wellbeing, and access to the Employee Assistance Program counseling sessions. HR will inform complainants of the case outcome in writing, subject to the respondent's privacy rights.

## Mandatory Training

All employees complete anti-harassment training during onboarding (within the first fifteen days) and an annual refresher each August. Managers and CODI members complete additional training on receiving and handling complaints. Completion is tracked in the learning portal and is a factor in performance reviews.
