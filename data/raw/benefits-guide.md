# Benefits Guide

## Health Insurance (HMO)

### Coverage

All regular employees are enrolled in the company HMO plan (Maxicare Platinum equivalent) effective on their regularization date. The plan includes a maximum benefit limit (MBL) of PHP 200,000 per illness per year, coverage at all major hospitals nationwide, and access to accredited clinics for outpatient consultations.

| Benefit | Coverage |
| --- | --- |
| Room and board | Private room, up to PHP 4,500/day |
| Outpatient consultation | Unlimited, accredited clinics |
| Annual physical exam | Included, once per year |
| Dental | 2 cleanings/year, basic procedures |
| Emergency care | Covered at any hospital, reimbursed at non-accredited |
| Maximum benefit limit | PHP 200,000 per illness per year |

### Dependents

After one (1) year of continuous service, employees may enroll up to two (2) qualified dependents (spouse, children aged 15 days to 23 years, or parents below 65 for single employees) at company cost. Additional dependents may be enrolled at the employee's expense through salary deduction. Dependent enrollment happens during the annual enrollment window each January, or within thirty (30) days of a qualifying life event (marriage, birth).

## Government-Mandated Benefits

The company remits all government-mandated contributions on your behalf: SSS (Social Security System), PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG (HDMF). Employee shares are deducted from payroll each cycle; the employer share is paid by the company. Certificates of contribution are available from HR on request. The company also processes SSS sickness, maternity, and salary loan applications.

## Group Life Insurance

Regular employees are covered by group term life insurance equal to twenty-four (24) times monthly basic salary, with accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D) coverage at an additional twelve (12) times monthly basic salary. Beneficiary designations are made during onboarding and may be updated any time through HR.

## Retirement Plan

The company operates a non-contributory retirement plan in compliance with RA 7641. Employees with at least five (5) years of service who retire at age sixty (60) or older receive one-half month salary per year of service, where "one-half month" includes 15 days pay, one-twelfth of the 13th month pay, and the cash equivalent of 5 days of service incentive leave. An optional voluntary provident fund with a 50% company match (up to 3% of monthly basic salary) is available after regularization.

## Allowances

| Allowance | Amount | Eligibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rice subsidy | PHP 2,000/month | All regular employees |
| Communication allowance | PHP 1,500/month | Roles requiring client contact; manager approval |
| Transportation allowance | PHP 2,500/month | On-site and hybrid employees |
| Internet allowance | PHP 1,000/month | Remote and hybrid employees |

Allowances are non-taxable up to the BIR de minimis thresholds; amounts above thresholds are taxed through payroll.

## Wellness Program

Employees may claim reimbursement of up to PHP 1,500 per month for gym memberships, fitness classes, or sports club fees. Submit official receipts through the HRIS expense module within sixty (60) days of payment. The company also provides free annual flu vaccination each July and access to a confidential Employee Assistance Program (EAP) offering six (6) free counseling sessions per year.

## Learning and Development Budget

Each regular employee has an annual learning budget of PHP 30,000 for courses, certifications, conferences, and books relevant to their role. Requests are submitted through the HRIS with manager approval. Certification exam fees for a failed attempt may be covered once per certification. Unused budget does not carry over to the next year.
