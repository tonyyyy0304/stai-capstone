# Employee Handbook

## About Lumina Works

Lumina Works Inc. is a technology services company headquartered in Bonifacio Global City, Taguig. Our values are candor, craft, and care: we speak plainly, we build things well, and we look after our people and clients. This handbook summarizes the standards of conduct expected of every employee; detailed policies (leave, benefits, remote work, grievance, anti-harassment) are published as separate documents and prevail over this summary where they differ.

## Code of Conduct

### Professional Behavior

Employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect and courtesy, meet commitments and deadlines, and communicate honestly. Prohibited conduct includes dishonesty in any work record, insubordination, intoxication at work, gambling on company premises, and any act that endangers others.

### Conflicts of Interest

Disclose to HR any outside employment, business interest, or family relationship that could conflict with your duties — including moonlighting for a competitor, supplier, or client. Accepting gifts from vendors or clients above PHP 2,000 in value must be declared; cash gifts of any amount must be declined. Annual conflict-of-interest declarations are collected each January.

### Confidentiality and Data Protection

Company information, client data, and personal data of colleagues must be handled per your signed NDA and the Data Privacy Act (RA 10173). Do not share credentials, forward company documents to personal accounts, or discuss confidential matters in public spaces. Obligations survive separation from the company.

## Work Hours and Attendance

The standard workweek is Monday to Friday, eight (8) working hours per day plus a one-hour unpaid lunch break. Core collaboration hours are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM; start times may flex between 7:00 AM and 10:00 AM with manager agreement. Employees must log time in and out through the HRIS. Tardiness beyond fifteen (15) minutes after your agreed start time is recorded; six (6) or more tardy instances in a month triggers a written reminder, and habitual tardiness is a disciplinable offense.

## Dress Code

Business casual applies on client-facing days and in client offices; smart casual applies otherwise. Fridays are casual. Regardless of day: no offensive prints, and closed footwear is required in laboratory and server room areas.

## Performance Management

Performance is reviewed on an annual cycle with a mid-year check-in. Ratings follow a five-point scale and calibrate across departments. Persistent underperformance is addressed through a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) of at least sixty (60) days with written goals, before any separation decision. Performance disputes may be raised through the grievance procedure.

## Disciplinary Process

Violations of company policy are handled with progressive discipline observing due process: a written Notice to Explain (NTE) giving the employee five (5) calendar days to respond, an administrative hearing when facts are contested, and a written Notice of Decision. Sanctions range from written warning to suspension (up to thirty 30 days) to dismissal, proportionate to the offense and prior record. Serious offenses — theft, fraud, violence, harassment, gross negligence — may warrant dismissal on the first offense and, where applicable, preventive suspension during investigation.

## Resignation and Separation

### Notice Period

Employees resigning must give at least thirty (30) calendar days written notice to their manager and HR, per Article 300 of the Labor Code. The company may waive part of the notice period at its discretion. Managers and employees holding critical roles are requested to give sixty (60) days where possible to allow turnover.

### Clearance and Exit

Complete the exit clearance in the HRIS: return of laptop, access cards, and company property; turnover documentation; and settlement of accountabilities. An exit interview with HR is scheduled during your last week. Final pay and BIR Form 2316 follow the timelines in the Payroll and Compensation FAQ.
